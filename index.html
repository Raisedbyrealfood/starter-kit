Step One

The 5-Minute Pantry Audit

Here are some examples of common things families are eating and some suggestions on healthier, real food swaps!

  • Flavoured yogurt pouches → Plain full-fat yogurt with honey or maple syrup, cinnamon, frozen fruit or fresh fruit & nut butter
  • Sugary cereal → Sourdough French toast with maple syrup & fruit. Oatmeal, maple syrup, berries & nut butter
  • Packaged granola bars → Fruit with nut butter, crackers and cheese, trail mix with nuts and dried fruit, or homemade granola bars
  • Vegetable oil or seed oil cooking spray → Butter, ghee, olive oil, coconut oil, tallow, duck fat, or avocado oil
  • Flavoured dipping sauces full of additives → Coconut aminos, Dijon mustard, honey, maple syrup, Greek yogurt, or a clean hot sauce
Know What You Are Buying

Reading Labels
Without the Overwhelm

Food labels are not designed to make your decision easy. They are designed to help the product sell. Once you understand that, the whole thing becomes so much simpler!

I used to buy things that said 'natural' or 'plant-based' on the front and never flip them over. The front of the package is marketing. The back is the truth. Once I started reading the back, I could not ignore what was in the food we were buying!

The Truth About Packaged Food
Seed oils are added because

They are cheap and extend shelf life. Not for your health. Not to nourish your family. For the company's bottom line. Seed oils are also highly processed and unstable at high heat, which means they oxidise easily and can promote inflammation in the body. Most of us are consuming far more of them than we realise because they are in almost every packaged food on the shelf.

Artificial Dyes

They make food look brighter and more appealing. They have no nutritional value whatsoever, and several of them have been linked to behavioural issues, hyperactivity, and allergic reactions in children. If you have a child who struggles with focus, mood swings, or hyperactivity, removing artificial dyes is one of the first things worth trying. Many parents notice a significant difference within days.

Sugar has 60+ names because

If sugar appeared as the first ingredient, you might not buy it. Splitting it across five names hides how much is actually in there. When you see two or three different sugar names in one product, add them together mentally. Combined they often make up the majority of what you are eating.

"Natural flavours" means

Almost anything derived from a plant or animal source. It is a broad category designed to make products more craveable, not more nutritious. The problem is you have no way of knowing exactly what is in it, which makes it very hard to make an informed choice about what you are feeding your family.

Your 10-Second Label Filter

You do not need to analyse every ingredient. Ask yourself these three questions and you will know what to buy and what to put back.

1
Do I recognise most of these ingredients?
If the ingredient list looks like something you could make at home, it is usually a good choice.
2
What are the first three ingredients?
Ingredients are listed by weight from most to least. The first three tell you what the product is mostly made of. If seed oils or sugar are in the top three, put it back.
3
Is this mostly food or mostly additives?
If it reads like a chemistry experiment, it probably is one. Real food ingredients are recognisable. Trust that instinct!
A Quick Example

Same product, two very different labels. You do not need to be an expert to see the difference!

Granola Bar A
First ingredients:
Rolled oats, almonds, honey, coconut oil
Put it in the cart!
Granola Bar B
First ingredients:
Oats, corn syrup, soybean oil, sugar, almonds, natural flavours
Put it back!
That quick comparison takes about ten seconds. You do not need to read every word. You just need to look at the beginning of the list and trust what you see!
Before You Start Cooking

The Simple Formulas
Behind Every Meal

You do not need a new recipe every night. You just need a simple formula and real ingredients. Once you know these, you can build a meal out of almost anything in your fridge!

Breakfast
Protein + Carb
Start the day with protein and it keeps kids (and you) full and focused for hours. Carbs alone spike and crash, protein steadies everything out.
Eggs + toast · Yogurt + fruit · Oats + nut butter · Sausage + eggs · French toast + turkey bacon
Snack
Protein or Fat + Carb
Carbs on their own digest fast and kids are hungry again in 30 minutes. Add a protein or fat and the snack actually lasts until the next meal. This one change makes dinner so much easier!
Fruit + nut butter · Cheese + crackers · Yogurt + berries · Meat + fruit · Rice crackers + nut butter or guacamole
Lunch
Protein + Carb + Fruit
Lunch does not need to be exciting. It needs to be filling and familiar. Kids eat more when lunch is predictable. Pick a few they love and rotate them all week. Repetition is a feature, not a flaw!
Sandwich + fruit · Snack plate · Leftover dinner · Wrap + veggies · Soup + toast + fruit · Yogurt/fruit smoothie
Dinner
Protein + Carb + Veg
Dinner does not need to be creative. It needs to be repeatable. Five dinners on rotation beats fifty recipes on Pinterest every time. Pick your five and make them until they are on autopilot!
Ground beef + rice + vegetables · Shepherds pie · Chicken + potatoes + veg · Pasta + meat sauce · Steak + squash + fruit · Stew · Chilli ·
The One Snack Rule That Changes Everything

Close the kitchen 2 hours before dinner and stick to it calmly. When kids come to the table actually hungry, they eat more willingly, they complain less, and they are far more likely to try something new.

A Note on Sweeteners

Eating real food is not about removing sugar from your family's life. It is about choosing sweeteners that are closer to their natural form. Raw honey and maple syrup still contain trace minerals and are far less processed than refined sugar. Dates are another great option as they are a whole food that sweeten naturally and come with fibre too. If you prefer something with no sugar at all, stevia and monk fruit are both plant-based sweeteners that do not spike blood sugar. Enjoy whichever works for your family, just choose them intentionally!

Keeping It Real

Real Food vs
Real Life

Can we just be honest for a second? Because most real food accounts will not say this and I think it actually needs to be said!

Sometimes real food does cost a little more.

Eggs, rice, potatoes, oats and frozen vegetables are generally affordable. But yes, grass-fed butter costs more than margarine. Good olive oil costs more than canola. I personally think it is worth it but I also want to be honest that there can be a real cost difference. The full course has an entire module on eating real food on any budget, because it is absolutely possible. You just need a strategy!

Sometimes it takes more time.

Scrambled eggs take longer than pouring cereal from a box. Roasting vegetables takes longer than opening a bag of chips. I am not going to pretend otherwise. What I will say is that the time investment gets smaller the more familiar it becomes, and the five-dinner rotation we talk about in the course makes meal times super simple!

Your kids will not always eat it.

Some nights you will make a real food dinner and someone will refuse it. That is just parenting. It has nothing to do with real food specifically. Picky eating is a whole topic we cover properly in the course, there are real strategies that actually work, but I want you to know going in that there will be hard nights. Those nights do not mean you are failing. They just mean you have kids!

You will not do it perfectly. And that is completely fine.

There will be days or weeks where real food just does not happen. A sick kid, a crazy work week, travelling, feeling burnt out.. Those weeks do not erase all the weeks before them. Real food is not a streak you have to protect. It is a baseline you come back to. And as long as you keep coming back, it counts!

I am not selling you a perfect life. I am just showing you a simpler, more nourishing way to feed your family, on real days, with real constraints, for real people!

When It All Falls Apart

Some nights cooking feels genuinely impossible. You are exhausted, the kids are melting down, nothing is prepped. This is not failure, this is real life and it happens to absolutely everyone at some point. Here are a few tips to handle those hectic nights you usually resort to ordering takeout. Don't get me wrong, takeout is great occasionally, but we are trying to stay consistent with real food until it becomes your default & new normal!

Tier 1. The 10-Minute Saves

Keep these ingredients stocked and dinner feels easy and stress free on those busy days!

Scrambled eggs + toast + fruit
Eggs are the best emergency dinner. High protein, five minutes, kids love them. Breakfast for dinner!
Rice + butter + fried egg
Leftover rice, a little butter, a fried egg on top. Genuinely delicious and ready in minutes.
Pasta + olive oil + parmesan
No sauce needed. Just good pasta, a drizzle of olive oil or butter, salt, and lots of parmesan or other cheese. Add a fried egg or meat on top for protein.
Shepherds pie
Ground beef in a little taco seasoning, mashed sweet potatoes, with butter and melted cheese on top. Delicious and ready in 20 minutes!
Tier 2. Your Freezer Is Your Best Friend

The best thing you can do for hard nights is stock your freezer when things are good!

Frozen burger patties
Frozen chicken thighs
Frozen meatballs
Frozen veggie bags
Pre-cooked rice portions
Frozen sausages
The most important thing to remember: the night falling apart does not undo anything. You do not restart and you definitely do not feel guilty! You just do your best (sometimes that means takeout) and tomorrow you come right back to real food, your new normal. The fact that you have a normal to return to, that is exactly what the goal is!

Questions I Get Asked All the Time

Honest Answers
To the Big Questions

These are the questions I get in my DMs on repeat. Here are my honest answers!

Is organic really worth it?

Sometimes yes, sometimes no. The Dirty Dozen, strawberries, spinach, apples, grapes, blueberries, and a few others, are worth buying organic when you can because they tend to have higher pesticide residues. The Clean Fifteen, avocados, pineapple, onions, cabbage, and others, are generally fine conventional. You do not need to buy everything organic to eat real food. That is one of the biggest myths out there and it makes real food feel way more expensive than it needs to be!

The full course covers exactly which organic swaps are worth the money and which ones are not, based on your budget.

My kids refuse everything. Where do I even start?

Start with what they already eat and build outward from there. If they eat pasta, start with pasta and a better sauce. If they eat rice, start with rice and a simple protein alongside it. You do not fight picky eating by replacing their safe foods. You build trust around mealtimes first. Picky eating is almost never about the food itself. It is about control, familiarity, and sensory comfort. And it responds incredibly well to calm, consistent, low-pressure meals. Not bribes, not negotiations, just calm and consistent!

There is a full picky eater module in the course with strategies that actually work!

Is this going to break my grocery budget?

It does not have to! The most affordable real foods are also some of the most nutritious ones, eggs, ground beef, rice, potatoes, oats, frozen vegetables, and seasonal fruit. The trap most people fall into is buying the "health version" of processed foods, organic crackers, gluten-free snacks, fancy protein bars. Those are expensive and they are still processed. Put that money toward more eggs, squash, rice, and meat and your budget will actually go further! When you can, buy in bulk if it's cheaper or on sale and freeze!

The course has a full budget module including where to shop, what to buy in bulk, and how to stretch proteins across multiple meals.

Do I have to give up everything my family loves?

Absolutely not! Real food is not about eliminating everything fun or making your family miserable at mealtimes. Pizza happens. Takeout happens. Birthday cake happens. The goal is not perfection. It is building a baseline that is real, nourishing, and sustainable on most days. When real food is your default, the occasional pizza night is just pizza night. It does not undo anything!

This is actually the whole first module of the course, breaking the all-or-nothing cycle for good!
